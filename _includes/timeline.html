What's Zombie Science?


Why won’t dead - and violent - ideas just die? Time and again we kill them, debunk them out of existence, but they keep coming back, deadlier than ever, and with alluring new names and claims. Our grandparents called it eugenics, but we call it biometrics. Our forefathers called it craniology and our aunties called it ___ and today the kids call it machine learning. In the 1960’s we thought criminality came from large Y chromosomes but in the 21st century we’re pretty sure it’s kept in the proportions of one’s face. We keep building gadgets to track people we consider risky or deviant, even as...

These zombie ideas are deadly. They are at the core of our violent racist, capitalist, hetero-patriarchical society. So why won’t these dead ideas just die? Because funders, institutions, and companies keep resurrecting them for profit. There are careers to be made in reviving zombie ideas, dealing in death is a profitable trade. And people refuse to learn from the lessons of the past. We keep acting like tired recycled ideas are radical innovations, when really it’s just more of the same.



The Undead Horde of Biometrics

1775

Blumenbach described five human varieties: Caucasians, Mongolians, Ethiopians, Americans, and Malays. Saini points out that "his arbitrary classification would have lasting consequences," as evidenced by the now-common use of "caucasian" to politely describe whiteness.

1891

A classic example of physiognomic literature, it really is remarkable how little this work has changed over the years

1902

This notable entrant to the physiognomic ring features characteristics the author thought were "genuine" and "unreliable." That dichotomy bears a striking resemblance to language used in modern facial recognition papers: "genuine" and "impostor."

2016

Two researchers claimed to have trained four classifiers to detect ~criminality~ "based solely on still face images, which is free of any biases of subjective judgments of human observers." Uh-huh. Yeah. Right.

2016

The pitch: "What if it was possible to know whether an anonymous individual is a potential terrorist, an aggressive person, or a potential criminal? Better yet, what if that information could be obtained and used in real-time, when it matters the most?"

2018

In 2018, two researchers created a deep neural network that they claimed could predict a person's sexuality (either gay or straight) based on their face. Turns out straight guys have goatees and straight women wear eyeliner.

Sept. 2020

In September 2020, researchers released the now-infamous facial "trustworthiness" paper. It's garbage for all sorts of reasons.
